# Summary

Cross-validated the C++ TileOp V2 encoder (`tile-cpp/build/run_tile`) against the Python TileOp V2 encoder (`tile-validator/process_tile`) on 1,000 off-axis tiles at the operating point around R=860M.

Result: **1,000 / 1,000 tiles matched byte-for-byte across the full 128-byte TileOp record.** No metadata mismatches, no TileOp status mismatches, no overflows, and no empty tiles were observed in the sample.

The group-bit steal h1 encoding also passed the secondary check. Across **25,627** live L/R ports, encode -> decode -> original `h1` round-tripped with **0 failures**. The sample included **110 tiles** with at least one `h1=256` L/R port, covering **113 total `h1=256` ports**.

# Method

I first read `tile-validator/cross_validate.py` to reuse its comparison contract:

- compare `prime_count`
- compare `group_count`
- compare `ports_before_pruning`
- compare `ports_after_pruning`
- compare all 128 TileOp bytes
- compare TileOp status
- verify group-bit steal round-trip on L/R ports

I rebuilt the C++ binary and validation tests:

- `cmake -S tile-cpp -B tile-cpp/build`
- `cmake --build tile-cpp/build --target run_tile test_face_encode test_e2e -j4`
- `./tile-cpp/build/test_face_encode`
- `./tile-cpp/build/test_e2e`

For the 1K sweep, I used a systematic off-axis sample instead of the near-axis census sweep. The sample consists of four 25x10 tile blocks centered on the 860M shell at approximately 15 deg, 30 deg, 45 deg, and 60 deg. All tile origins are multiples of 256 and all satisfy `sqrt(a_lo^2 + b_lo^2) >= 800,000,000`.

Block centers:

| Angle | Center `(a_lo, b_lo)` | Radius |
|---|---:|---:|
| 15 deg | `(830696192, 222584320)` | `859,999,966.81` |
| 30 deg | `(744781824, 429999872)` | `859,999,915.86` |
| 45 deg | `(608111872, 608111872)` | `860,000,056.82` |
| 60 deg | `(430000128, 744781824)` | `860,000,043.86` |

Across all 1,000 tiles, the sampled radius range was:

## Group-bit steal h1 verification

The validator's secondary round-trip check passed on every live L/R port:

| Metric | Value |
|---|---:|

The `h1=256` edge case encoded exactly as intended in both implementations:

- `group_byte = 0x80 | group_id`
- `h1_byte = 0x00`
- decoded `h1 = 256`

These examples confirm the stolen top bit is being used consistently by both encoders and that decode restores the original `h1` correctly.
